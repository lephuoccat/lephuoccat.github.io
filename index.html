About Me
I earned a B.S. degree Summa Cum Laude in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Rutgers University in 2016, followed by an M.S. degree in Electrical Engineering from the California Institute of Technology (Caltech) in 2017, and a Ph.D. in Machine Learning from Duke University in 2023. During my doctoral studies at Duke, I conducted pioneering research on Task Affinity and its applications in Machine Learning under the mentorship of Dr. Vahid Tarokh. I currently serve as an AI Safety Scientist at UL Solutions, where I develop and apply advanced machine learning techniques to ensure safe and reliable AI systems in real-world applications.
My research interests span Machine Learning, Computer Vision, and Natural Language Processing, with a particular focus on Transfer Learning, Few-Shot Learning, Continual Learning, Multi-Task Learning, and Neural Architecture Search. Throughout my academic career, I have been recognized with numerous honors, including membership in the Matthew Leydt Society, Outstanding Engineering Scholar, John B. Smith Award, Nikola Tesla Scholar, and the E. M. Toomey Scholarship. I am also an active member of the Tau Beta Pi, Eta Kappa Nu, and Sigma Alpha Pi honor societies, reflecting my commitment to excellence and leadership in both engineering and research.
